How Secure Rooms Work

1

Connect & Identify

Client opens a WebSocket connection to /ws/chat?room=NAME&username=USER. The server creates or joins the room and assigns a 256-bit AES key.

2

Encrypt & Send

Each message is AES-256-CBC encrypted with a fresh random IV. The HMAC-SHA256 is computed over the ciphertext for integrity. The wire never carries plaintext.

3

Broadcast & Decrypt

The server decrypts the incoming message, verifies the HMAC, then re-encrypts for each recipient in the room before forwarding. Each recipient decrypts to see the plaintext.

Electronic Voting (Homomorphic — Paillier)

Paillier homomorphic encryption allows tallying votes without ever decrypting individual ballots. The property: E(a) · E(b) = E(a + b). Cast your vote below — the encrypted tally updates but individual votes remain private.

How votes stay private:
• Each vote is encrypted before submission
• Encrypted votes are multiplied (homomorphic add)
• Only the final sum is decrypted — never individual votes
